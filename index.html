Betty Friedan

The mother of the modern women's liberation movement.

Here is a time line for Betty Friedan's life:

  • 1921 - Born in Peoria, Illinois, U.S.
  • 1938 - She attended all-female Smith College and won a scholarship prize in her first year for outstanding academic performance.
  • 1947 - Betty Goldstein married Carl Friedan, a theater director who later became an advertising executive.
  • 1957 - Friedan started research that was to have far-reaching results. Her class at Smith College was to gather for the fifteenth anniversary of their graduation.
  • 1963 - "The Feminine Mystique" book was published
  • 1966 - She helped establish NOW, the National Organization for Women.
  • 1970 -Betty Friedan led a huge demonstration in New York City for women's rights.
  • 1971 - The House of Representatives approved the Equal Rights Amendment
  • 1981 - wrote about the condition of the women's movement. Her book was called "The Second Stage."
  • 1993 - She wrote a book called "The Fountain of Age"
  • 2000 - Friedan's last book was published, Its title was "Life So Far."
  • 2006 - died at the age 85

"She helps make it better for women to feel good about being women, and therefore she helped make it possible for women to more freely love men."

-- Betty Friedan
